Sara Melchionda

Graduate Student | Public and Political Communication

I am a Master’s degree student in Public and Political Communication at the University of Turin. My research investigates the intersection of digital sociology and NLP, specifically focusing on the detection of polarization and sarcasm within online conspiratorial discourse. My current thesis project explores whether polarization in online communities is a genuine social shift or a methodological artifact. I leverage LLMs (BERT) to decode vernacular internet slang, using large-scale datasets retrieved from Reddit.

Skills & Methods

  • Quantitative Research: Data mining via community archives (Arctic Shift), Social Network Analysis (SNA).
  • Tools: Proficient in R for data wrangling; experienced with Stata.
  • Languages: Italian (Native), English (C1 - Cambridge Certified).
